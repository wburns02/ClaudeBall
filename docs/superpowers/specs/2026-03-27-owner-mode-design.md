# Claude Ball — Owner Mode Design Spec

**Date:** 2026-03-27
**Status:** Approved
**Depends on:** Dynasty Mode (complete), Career Progression System (complete)

---

## 1. Overview

Owner Mode is the endgame of the Living Dynasty career pipeline. After accumulating $500M+ in net worth and finding a team for sale, the player purchases a franchise and gameplay shifts to an ownership perspective with three distinct styles: Hands-Off, Active, or Maniac (owner-GM-manager).

A deep scandal and drama system drives emergent narratives — players have a hidden Wildcard trait that can explode into minor incidents or catastrophic league-altering events. The player themselves can be swept up in scandals through a chain of bad decisions.

### Design Principles

- **Ownership is earned, not given.** You can't buy a team that isn't for sale. You wait.
- **Every hat has a cost.** Wearing multiple roles saves money but damages reputation.
- **Scandals are consequences, not random.** Nuclear events require a chain of player choices.
- **Winning redeems everything.** A World Series as owner-manager erases the circus narrative.

## 2. Ownership Acquisition

### Prerequisites

- Net worth ≥ $500M (tracked by PersonalFinancesComponent)
- CareerProgressionSystem generates "Owner" opportunity ONLY when a team is for sale

### What Makes a Team Go For Sale

**Financial triggers:**
- Team loses money for 3+ consecutive seasons
- Owner patience meter (from DynastySettings) hits zero

**Story-driven events (generated by LifeEventSystem for NPC owners):**
- Owner health crisis (age 70+, probability increases yearly)
- Family dispute (heir doesn't want the team)
- Relocation drama (city won't fund new stadium)
- League pressure (incompetent owner forced out)

**Scandal-driven:**
- Owner caught in nuclear-tier scandal → league forces sale

**Player-influenced:**
- As rival GM: tank a team's value by trading away their stars when you face them in deals
- Build relationship (affinity > 60) with aging owner → they offer you first right of refusal
- Sabotage: spread rumors (low integrity action) → accelerates owner frustration

**Frequency guarantee:** At least one team goes for sale every 5 seasons. If RNG hasn't triggered a sale, the oldest/poorest owner sells.

### Purchase Options

- **Buy your current team:** 30% discount (you know the organization). Same roster, same staff.
- **Buy any team for sale:** Market price = `marketSize × rosterQuality × stadiumAge factor`. Range: $400M (small market dumpster fire) to $2B (large market contender).
- **No team for sale:** You wait. Keep your current role (GM, President, etc.). The game generates sale opportunities over time.

## 3. Three Ownership Styles

Selected at purchase, changeable anytime during the season.

| Style | You Control | Delegated To | Who You Hire |
|-------|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Hands-Off** | Annual budget, hire/fire GM | Everything | GM only |
| **Active** | Budget, approve/deny major trades and FA signings, hire/fire all staff | Day-to-day roster, lineup, in-game | GM + Manager + Coaches |
| **Maniac** | Everything — you ARE the GM and optionally the manager | Nothing | Coaches only (or nobody) |

**Switching:** Escalate anytime (Hands-Off → Active → Maniac). De-escalate requires hiring someone for the vacated role.

---

## 4. Multi-Hat System (Reputation-Driven)

Each role you personally fill saves its salary but carries reputation consequences.

| Configuration | Annual Savings | Rep Impact | Agent Impact |
|--------------|---------------|-----------|-------------|
| Owner only | $0 | Neutral | Normal |
| Owner + GM | $3-5M | Media -15, Fan -10 | Agents demand +10% premium |
| Owner + GM + Manager | $4-8M | Media -30, Fan -25 | Free agents avoid you, "CIRCUS" headlines |

### Winning Erases the Penalty

| Achievement | Rep Recovery |
|------------|-------------|
| 5-game win streak | -25% current penalty |
| Playoff appearance | -50% penalty |
| World Series appearance | Penalty → 0, media narrative flips |
| **World Series WIN as owner-manager** | **Prestige +50, "The Madman" achievement** |

### AI Conversation Shifts

When wearing multiple hats, conversations change dynamically:
- Reporters: hostile questions → grudging respect → awe (based on win record)
- Agents: refuse calls → negotiate coldly → "maybe he's onto something"
- Players: "this guy is insane" texts → "he actually knows the game" → loyalty bonus
- Rival GMs: mock you → respect you → fear you

## 5. Scandal & Drama System

### New Personality Trait: Wildcard (20-80)

- **Hidden** from the player until it manifests (first incident reveals it)
- Generated at player creation: `Wildcard = max(20, 100 - Integrity + (Ego * 0.3) + (Aggression * 0.2) + random(-10, 10))`
- High-talent + high-wildcard = the most dangerous combination
- **Coachable:** Mentoring + team culture + sports psychologist reduces Wildcard by 3-8 per season
- **Ignored:** Wildcard increases by 1-3 per season if player has low morale or bad influences

### Scandal Generation

Each offseason and mid-season checkpoint, every entity with Wildcard > 50 rolls:
- Probability per check: `(wildcard - 50) / 200`
- Example: Wildcard 70 = 10% chance per check, 2 checks per season = ~19% annual probability

### Scandal Tiers

**Tier 1 — Minor (Wildcard 50-60):**
- Bar fight, Twitter/social media rant, skipping practice, caught at nightclub
- 5-10 game suspension
- Rep: clubhouse -3, media -5, fan -3
- Conversation: manager talks to player, player apologizes or doubles down

**Tier 2 — Moderate (Wildcard 55-70):**
- DUI, substance abuse (alcohol/drugs), on-field brawl with fan, caught-on-camera incident
- 30-60 game suspension
- Rep: all meters -15
- Options: rehab program (miss 30 days, Wildcard -10) or deny (50% chance it escalates)
- AI conversation: agent calls frantically, press conference required

**Tier 3 — Severe (Wildcard 65-80):**
- Gambling on games, PED positive, domestic violence charge, financial fraud
- 81-162 game suspension (up to full season)
- Rep: all meters -30
- May be released by team (AI GM decision based on player value vs scandal severity)
- Press conference: league commissioner announces suspension
- Contract implications: team can void remaining years

**Tier 4 — Nuclear (Wildcard 75-80 + decision chain):**

Nuclear scandals are **NEVER random.** They require the player to make a series of bad choices:

**The Chain (5 decision points over 1-3 seasons):**

1. **The Invitation** (only triggers if Integrity < 35 AND Wildcard > 65)
   - Life event: "You've been invited to an exclusive party by a wealthy acquaintance."
   - Choice: Go (chain continues) / Skip (chain breaks, Integrity +2)

2. **The Introduction**
   - Life event: "Your host introduces you to some influential people. They have a business proposition."
   - Choice: Take the meeting (chain continues) / Politely decline (chain breaks, Integrity +2)

3. **The Offer**
   - Life event: "The deal sounds too good to be true. Your gut says something's off."
   - AI conversation: Agent warns you. "I don't like these people. Walk away."
   - Choice: Proceed (chain continues) / Walk away (chain breaks, Integrity +3)

4. **The Trip**
   - Life event: "There's an exclusive retreat. Private jet. No phones allowed."
   - Choice: Go (chain continues) / Refuse (chain breaks, Integrity +5, Wildcard -5)

5. **The Point of No Return**
   - Life event: "You're in too deep. You've seen things you can't unsee."
   - No choice. The story will break in 6-12 game months.

**Nuclear Fallout:**
- **Lifetime ban from baseball**
- If owner: forced sale, proceeds partially seized
- Prestige → 0, all milestones stripped
- Every relationship → -100
- "BANNED" stamp on career page permanently
- AI generates: press conference, ESPN segment, teammates distancing, sponsors pulling out
- The game continues — but your career is over. Start a new dynasty or watch from the outside.

### Managing Other People's Scandals (Owner/GM Perspective)

As owner or GM, you face decisions about YOUR players' scandals:

**Scouting Wildcard:**
- Scouts report "character concerns" on prospects (visible Wildcard range, not exact number)
- Draft decision: take the risk on talent or pass on the time bomb?

**Incident Response:**
- Player gets DUI → press conference: support publicly (fan rep -10) or condemn (player relationship -20)?
- Star player's Wildcard climbing → invest in sports psychologist ($500K/year) or hope for the best?
- Manager caught gambling → fire (instability) or suspend (media calls you soft)?

**The Cover-Up Option (Low Integrity only):**
- If your Integrity < 40, you get the option to cover up moderate scandals
- 60% chance it works (scandal disappears, no suspension)
- 40% chance it leaks and BOTH of you get punished (your rep -25 on top of the original scandal)

## 6. Owner Dashboard (New Page)

Route: `/dynasty/owner`

### Sections:

**Org Chart:**
- Visual hierarchy: Owner → GM → Manager → Coaches (Hitting, Pitching, Bench, Bullpen) → Scouts (3-5)
- Each NPC has: name, personality traits, competence rating (1-100), salary, years on contract
- Click to hire/fire. Firing costs buyout. Hiring shows candidate pool with interview conversations.
- **"I'll Do It Myself" button** on GM and Manager slots — wear the hat yourself

**Financials:**
- Revenue: tickets (attendance × price), TV deal (fixed + performance bonus), merchandise, stadium naming rights, postseason bonus
- Expenses: payroll, staff salaries, facilities, scouting budget, marketing
- Profit/Loss with trend chart
- Franchise value (updates seasonally based on wins, market, revenue)

**Hat Selector:**
- Toggle: Owner only / Owner+GM / Owner+GM+Manager
- Shows current salary savings and reputation penalty side-by-side
- Warning when stacking: "This will generate negative media coverage. Proceed?"

**Scandal Monitor:**
- List of all players with visible Wildcard > 50
- Current incidents: status (investigating / suspended / resolved), action buttons
- Historical scandal log for the franchise
- "Invest in Culture" button — allocate budget to team-wide Wildcard reduction

**League Landscape:**
- Franchise values across the league (bar chart)
- Teams potentially for sale (flagged with indicators)
- Your relationship with each owner (from RelationshipSystem)

## 7. Technical Integration

### New Components
- `OwnershipComponent`: team owned, purchase price, franchise value, ownership style, hats worn
- Extend `PersonalityComponent`: add `wildcard` trait (20-80)

### New/Modified Systems
- Extend `CareerProgressionSystem`: add ownership acquisition logic, team-for-sale generation
- Extend `LifeEventSystem`: add scandal chain events, cover-up decisions
- New `ScandalSystem`: Wildcard checks, scandal generation, consequence application
- Extend `ReputationSystem`: multi-hat penalty/recovery logic
- Extend `PrestigeEngine`: ownership achievements, scandal impact on legacy

### New UI Pages
- `DynastyOwnerPage`: org chart, financials, hat selector, scandal monitor
- Modify `DynastyCareerTransitionPage`: show "Buy Team" option when available

### Event Bus Events
- `TeamForSale`: { teamId, reason, askingPrice }
- `TeamPurchased`: { buyerId, teamId, price }
- `ScandalOccurred`: { entityId, tier, type, description }
- `ScandalResolved`: { entityId, outcome }
- `HatChanged`: { entityId, hats: string[] }
- `OwnerForced toSell`: { entityId, reason }

---

## 8. Success Criteria

1. Player can buy a team when one is for sale and they have $500M+
2. Three ownership styles work with distinct gameplay for each
3. Multi-hat salary savings and reputation penalties function correctly
4. Winning as owner-manager triggers achievement and prestige boost
5. Wildcard trait generates scandals at appropriate frequency
6. Nuclear scandal chain requires 4+ deliberate bad choices
7. Scandal fallout cascades correctly (ban, forced sale, prestige reset)
8. Owner Dashboard shows org chart, financials, and scandal monitor
9. AI conversations shift based on ownership style and win record
10. At least one team goes for sale every 5 seasons
